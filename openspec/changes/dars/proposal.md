## The Problem

260 million children are out of school. Millions more sit in classrooms but aren't learning. In India, half of Grade 5 students cannot read a Grade 2 text.

The children who need tutoring most are the ones who can least afford it.

Current solutions fail because they assume:
- High-end smartphones ($500+)
- Reliable internet
- Parents who can pay $20-50/month
- English fluency

These assumptions exclude the students who need help most.

## The Solution: Chat-First (Telegram â†’ WhatsApp)

### Phase 0: Telegram

**Why Telegram for validation?**
- **Zero API cost** - completely free, no per-message fees
- **Instant deployment** - create bot via @BotFather in minutes, no approval process
- **Rich features** - inline keyboards, formatting, file sharing built-in
- **Growing in India** - 2nd most popular messenger, especially among students
- **Lightweight** - works well on budget Android phones
- **Proves the hypothesis** - validates "will students engage with AI tutoring via chat?"

**The tradeoff:** Students must install Telegram (free, one-time friction).

### Phase 1+: WhatsApp Business API

Once we have engagement data and user testimonials from Phase 0, we can:
- Apply for WhatsApp Business API with proof of traction
- Partner with a Business Solution Provider (Wati, Gupshup, etc.)
- Migrate successful students to WhatsApp for broader reach

This lets us **start today** instead of waiting weeks for WhatsApp approval.

### Cost Control

1. **Claude Haiku for most interactions** - 20x cheaper than Sonnet
2. **Aggressive prompt caching** - same math explanations reused
3. **Pre-generated content** - common problems cached, not generated live
4. **Rate limiting** - 10 AI interactions/day max

### Content Strategy

**Phase 0: Math only, Grade 6-8**

| Source | Approach |
|--------|----------|
| Problem sets | Curate from open educational resources (Khan Academy, OpenStax) |
| Explanations | Pre-generate with Claude, human review |
| Localization | Translate to Bengali, contextualize (mangoes not apples, rupees not dollars) |
| Curriculum alignment | Map to West Bengal Board of Secondary Education (WBBSE) curriculum |

**Content is NOT AI-generated on the fly.** We curate and cache. AI is used for:
- Adaptive hint generation (Socratic responses)
- Explaining where a student went wrong
- Personalized encouragement
